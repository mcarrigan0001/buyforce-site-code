Why buying centers fail

Buying directly from consumers is one of the smartest plays in used cars. It's also one of the worst executed by dealers, until now.

Six breakdowns kill most buying centers before they ever bear fruit. BuyForce was built around fixing every one of them.

01

Finding cars eats your reps alive.

Sourcing is slow and tedious. Reps burn hours digging through listings, filtering out dealers posing as private sellers, and chasing cars that already sold.

BuyForce finds the right cars based on your acquisition parameters and delivers them into the platform, ready for conversations and offers.

02

No VIN, no value, no offer.

Most private listings have no VIN. Asking the seller for it adds a step, costs time, lowers response rates, and kills deals before an offer is ever made.

Plate-to-VIN. A plate number from the listing photo becomes a fully decoded vehicle in seconds. No extra ask, no waiting.

03

Your team sells cars. Buying them is different.

Dealers know the retail process inside and out, but private party acquisition is a different discipline, and most teams do not know where to start.

The platform guides your team quickly and seamlessly through the high-converting acquisition process, end to end.

04

Appraisals sit. Deals die.

Managers and appraisers carry long to-do lists. Sight-unseen values on cars not on the lot fall to the bottom, and deals go cold waiting for a number.

Appraisers are notified immediately and reps are notified the moment values are ready, so offers go out while sellers are still engaged.

05

You are competing with household names.

CarMax and Carvana have massive budgets, seamless processes, and logistical supremacy. Standing out gets harder every year.

BuyForce makes your store the most credible, most compelling option with a standout offer presentation no local competitor can match.

06

Follow-up falls through the cracks.

Sellers rarely say yes on the first touch. Reps juggle dozens of opportunities, and good deals quietly go cold without structured follow-up.

Built-in cadences keep every opportunity top of mind and tell reps what to say and when. Nothing slips.

The platform

One screen. The whole deal.

From only having a plate number from a listing photo, to sight-unseen appraisal, offer, and negotiation, to arranging logistics and securing a signed purchase, the entire acquisition workflow lives on one screen. No spreadsheets, no guesswork, no deal-killing back-and-forth with the desk.

The competitive edge

Know you have the winning number before the conversation starts.

Most private sellers already know CarMax and Carvana's number before you talk to them. BuyForce shows your appraiser how much it will likely take to get the car. It also shows your rep what those offers are and confirms yours beats them, before the conversation happens. When the seller sees it side by side on the screen, the conversation changes.

The offer sheet

While other buyers send a number, you send a case.

Most of these sellers are contacted on Facebook, not in person. The moment the appraisal is ready, a branded offer sheet is generated and sent to the seller. Branded with your store's name, not ours.

It reinforces your offer amount, helps them consider how little they stand to gain by selling privately the hard way, adds credibility to your rep and your company, and explains how easy it is to sell to you. It also surfaces the vehicle's accident history and condition, so your number makes sense to them.
